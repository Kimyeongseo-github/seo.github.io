Patrick Young

I am a lecturer in the Standford Computer Science Department. Before coming to Standford, I spent two years as a programmer at Taligent (now part of IBM). At Taligent, I worked on Places for Project Teams, a groupware product designed to improve communication and coordination within small project team. I was fortunate to work with the Places for Project Teams product throughout an entire software lifecycle¤Ñfrom initial product conception conception through the actual product launch.

I have a Ph.D. from the University of California, Irvine, where I specialized in Software Engineering. My dissertation was on process support for software engineering teams. I also have a masters degree from Irvine, with a specializaion in Artificial Intelligence, and bachelors degrees in Computer Science and Eletrical Engineering.

>At Irvine, I taught Introduction to Software Engineering, a sophomore core class, and Software Processes, a graduate seminar. I also worked as a peer academic advisor, a freshmen orientation advisor, and served on leadership for campus chapters of both Habitat for Humanity and Inter Varsity Christian Felloship.
